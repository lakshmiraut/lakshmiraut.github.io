Della had one dollar and 87 cents. That was all. Almost half of it was in pennies. Pennies saved one or two at a time. When she bought her fruit and meat, she argued with the grocers for lower prices. When she paid, she always checked the change carefully. Now she counted the money she had saved three times. It was the same each time. She only had one dollar and 87 cents. And the next day would be Christmas.

She could think of only one thing to do. She lay down on the couch and cried. Her apartment did not offer her much comfort, since the rent was only eight dollars a week.

It is difficult to describe the apartment because there is not much to describe. The rooms were almost empty. They held few pieces of furniture, and the furniture was old.

There was an electric doorbell next to the front door of the building. The doorbell was dead and useless.
