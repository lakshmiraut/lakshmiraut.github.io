A few minutes later, she stood in front of a store. The sign above it read, “Mrs. Sofronie. All Types of Hair Products.” Della opened the door and ran upstairs. Mrs. Sofronie was large, pale, and unfriendly.

“Will you buy my hair?” asked Della breathlessly.

“Hair is my business,” said the lady. “Take off yer hat, and let’s have a look at yer hair.”

Della took off her hat. The shiny brown silk flowed over her shoulders.

Mrs. Sofronie touched the hair like an expert.

“I will give you 20 dollars,” she said.

“Give it to me quickly,” said Della.

The next two hours passed like a happy dream while Della looked in all the stores for Jim’s present.

Finally, she found it. It was perfect. It was the only one of its kind, simple and elegant-a platinum chain for Jim’s gold watch. The chain had a quiet nobility, like Jim. After Della paid 21 dollars for it, she only had 87 cents left.

As she hurried home, she said to herself: “Jim’s watch will look even more beautiful with the shiny chain.”

When she arrived home, she lit the gas stove and got out her curling irons. She sat in front of the mirror and began to fix her hair.

After 40 minutes, her short hair was full of curls like a schoolboy. She looked carefully at her reflection in the mirror.

“If Jim doesn’t kill me,” she said to herself, “he’ll say that I look like a girl in a cheap carnival. But I had to sell my hair! I only had one dollar and 87 cents!”
